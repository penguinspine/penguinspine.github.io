What is the z-score?


The z-score, also known as the standard score, z-value, or normal score, is a unitless measure used to express the signed, fractional deviation of an event from the mean of the measured values in terms of standard deviations. Positive z-scores indicate values above the mean, while negative z-scores indicate values below the mean.


To compute the z-score, one subtracts the population mean from the raw score or data point (such as a test score, height, age, etc.) and then divides this difference by the population standard deviation:


z= x−μ/σ


Here, x is the raw score, μ is the population mean, and σ is the population standard deviation. For a sample, a similar formula is used, with the sample mean and sample standard deviation replacing the population mean and population standard deviation. The z-score finds diverse applications, including z-tests, prediction interval calculations, utilization in process control, comparison of scores on different scales, and more.
